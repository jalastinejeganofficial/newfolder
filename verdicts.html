Verdict Classification System

Understanding our four-tier fact-checking verdict system. Each verdict provides transparent reasoning, confidence scoring, and evidence-based analysis to help you make informed decisions about information credibility.

Accurate / True

Claim is factually correct and supported by strong, credible evidence from multiple trusted sources.

Partially Misleading

Contains partial truth but is deceptive, incomplete, or taken out of context in significant ways.

False

Claim contradicts well-established facts and evidence from authoritative, verified sources.

Unverifiable

Insufficient evidence exists to confirm or deny the claim with current available information.

Detailed Verdict Breakdown

Accurate / True

VERIFIED

Claims rated as Accurate or True have been verified against multiple high-quality sources and are supported by strong empirical evidence, expert consensus, or official documentation.

Criteria:

  • Supported by peer-reviewed research
  • Confirmed by government or academic sources
  • Consistent with established scientific consensus
  • Verified by multiple independent fact-checkers
  • No significant contradictory evidence exists
Example Claim

"Water boils at 100°C (212°F) at sea level atmospheric pressure."

Partially Misleading

CAUTION

These claims contain elements of truth but omit crucial context, use selective framing, or present information in a way that creates a distorted or incomplete picture.

Characteristics:

  • Technically true but lacks important context
  • Uses cherry-picked data or statistics
  • Presents correlation as causation
  • Outdated information presented as current
  • Exaggerates or overstates findings
Example Claim

"Vaccines cause side effects" (True, but omits that serious side effects are extremely rare, while benefits vastly outweigh risks)

False

DEBUNKED

False claims have been definitively contradicted by overwhelming evidence from authoritative sources and expert consensus. These claims often originate from misinformation or disinformation campaigns.

Indicators:

  • Contradicts peer-reviewed scientific research
  • Debunked by multiple fact-checking organizations
  • Based on fabricated or manipulated data
  • Rejected by subject matter experts
  • Origins trace to unreliable or biased sources
Example Claim

"The Earth is flat and NASA is hiding the truth."

Unverifiable

INSUFFICIENT DATA

Unverifiable claims lack sufficient available evidence to make a determination. This may be due to emerging topics, limited data, or claims about private/internal matters.

When Used:

  • Emerging events with limited reliable reporting
  • Claims about future predictions or speculation
  • Private or internal matters without documentation
  • Anecdotal evidence without corroboration
  • Topics requiring specialized expertise not available
Example Claim

"This new supplement will cure all diseases within 5 years."
